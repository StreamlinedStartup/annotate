Live demo

Review anything, right on the page.

This is a real, working demo of annotate.js. Look at the floating toolbar in the bottom-right corner — pick a tool and start marking up this page.

Try it: select any sentence to highlight it, press R to draw a rectangle, or hover a paragraph and click the + button that appears in the margin. Open the comments panel with A, then hit Download to export your notes as JSON.

Why review on the live page?

Screenshots go stale, and describing "the third button under the hero" in a chat thread is painful. annotate.js lets reviewers point at the exact pixel and text they mean — highlights, boxes, pins and sketches all anchor to real content, even as the page reflows.

"Move this heading up, it competes with the logo." — a comment that only makes sense when it's attached to the heading.

Everything stays in your browser

There is no server here. Every comment you create on this page is saved in your browser's localStorage. Refresh the page and your notes are still there. When you're ready to share, export a small JSON file and send it to a teammate — they import it and see your feedback in place.

Highlight text

Select any words and a comment popover appears instantly.

Draw & pin

Rectangles, circles, freehand ink and point pins for visual notes.

Threaded replies

Discuss each note, then resolve it when it's handled.

Download & import

Round-trip your review as a portable JSON file. No accounts.

Add it to your own site

One line, right before the closing </body> tag:

<script src="https://cdn.jsdelivr.net/npm/@annotatejs/core/annotate.js" defer></script>

That's the entire installation. See the README for React, Vue, WordPress and more.
